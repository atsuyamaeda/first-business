## 1. Commercial Lease Costs (10-30 tsubo / 33-100 sq meters)

### Monthly Rent by Area (per tsubo / month)

| Area | Rent/tsubo/month (JPY) | Monthly Rent (15 tsubo) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Koenji (高円寺)** | 15,000-25,000 | 225,000-375,000 | Most affordable of target areas; indie/subculture vibe |
| **Shimokitazawa (下北沢)** | 20,000-35,000 | 300,000-525,000 | Gentrified post-redevelopment; popular cafe street area |
| **Sangenjaya (三軒茶屋)** | 18,000-30,000 | 270,000-450,000 | Local feel; Setagaya-ku; growing specialty cafe scene |
| **Kichijoji (吉祥寺)** | 20,000-35,000 | 300,000-525,000 | High foot traffic; outside 23-ku so slightly cheaper |
| **Nakameguro (中目黒)** | 25,000-45,000 | 375,000-675,000 | Trendy; high-end clientele; fashion/lifestyle brands |
| **Daikanyama (代官山)** | 30,000-50,000 | 450,000-750,000 | Premium area; luxury positioning; lower foot traffic |
| **Shibuya (渋谷)** | 30,000-60,000 | 450,000-900,000 | Highest rents; 40,000-60,000 for center/Dogenzaka |

### Key Notes
- **Shibuya station area** was ranked 5th nationally at avg 44,362 JPY/tsubo in 2024 H1
- Nakameguro/Meguro area saw +59.2% rent increase in recent years due to gentrification
- Small spaces (under 15 tsubo) tend to have higher per-tsubo rates
- Side streets and shotengai (shopping streets) are 20-40% cheaper than main road-facing (romenten) locations
- **Realistic monthly rent for a 10-20 tsubo matcha cafe: 250,000-500,000 JPY/month** in the mid-tier areas

### Floor-Level Rent Differences (Tokyo-Specific)

| Floor | Relative Cost vs 1F | Example (20 tsubo, base 1F = 30,000/tsubo) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1F (路面店 / street level)** | 100% (baseline) | 600,000/month | Best visibility; walk-in traffic; premium |
| **B1F (地下)** | 40-60% of 1F | 240,000-360,000/month | Significant savings; needs signage strategy; common for bars/cafes |
| **2F (空中階)** | 50-70% of 1F | 300,000-420,000/month | Cheaper; lower walk-in traffic; needs destination appeal |

A real-world example: If 1F rent is 20,000/tsubo, 2F can be as low as 8,000/tsubo and B1F around 5,000-6,000/tsubo. However, 1F shops generate 1.8x+ more revenue than 2F on weekdays, meaning the rent savings can be offset by lower sales.

## 2. Property Acquisition Costs (物件取得費)

### Security Deposit, Key Money, and Related Costs

| Item | Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **保証金 (hoshokin / security deposit)** | 6-12 months' rent (standard); up to 24 months for prime locations | Refundable (minus deductions). 6-8 months increasingly common. |
| **礼金 (reikin / key money)** | 1-2 months' rent | Non-refundable "gift" to landlord |
| **仲介手数料 (broker fee)** | 1 month's rent + tax | Standard |
| **前家賃 (advance rent)** | 1-2 months' rent | First month + sometimes partial month |
| **火災保険 (fire insurance)** | 50,000-100,000/year | Required |
| **保証会社 (guarantor company)** | 0.5-1 month's rent | If no personal guarantor; increasingly required |

### Realistic Property Acquisition Budget
For a space at 400,000 JPY/month rent:
- Security deposit (8 months): **3,200,000**
- Key money (2 months): **800,000**
- Broker fee (1 month + tax): **440,000**
- Advance rent (1 month): **400,000**
- Fire insurance: **80,000**
- Guarantor company: **400,000**
- **Total upfront property costs: approx. 5,320,000 JPY (~$35,000 USD)**

## 3. Build-Out / Renovation Costs (内装工事費)

### Skeleton (スケルトン) vs.居抜き (Existing Build-Out)

| Type | Cost/tsubo (JPY) | 15 tsubo Total | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| **居抜き (ikemuki) - minimal** | 100,000-200,000 | 1,500,000-3,000,000 | Previous cafe; reuse existing kitchen/counter; cosmetic refresh |
| **居抜き (ikemuki) - moderate** | 200,000-350,000 | 3,000,000-5,250,000 | Partial renovation; new flooring, paint, lighting, some equipment |
| **スケルトン (skeleton) - standard** | 350,000-570,000 | 5,250,000-8,550,000 | Full build from bare concrete; median 571,000/tsubo for cafes |
| **スケルトン (skeleton) - high-end** | 570,000-800,000 | 8,550,000-12,000,000 | Custom design, premium materials, specialty fixtures |

### Key Notes
- **Cafe industry median for skeleton build-out: 571,000 JPY/tsubo** (2025 data)
- 居抜き物件 can save 50-70% vs. skeleton if the previous tenant was a similar business
- 2025 construction costs are elevated due to ongoing labor shortages (skilled carpenter shortage) and materials inflation
- Plumbing and gas work are the most expensive single items
- Design/architecture fees: typically 10-15% of construction cost
- **Realistic range for a 15 tsubo matcha cafe: 3,000,000-8,000,000 JPY** depending on ikemuki vs. skeleton

## 5. Permits and Licenses (資格・許可)

### Required (必須)

| Permit/License | Cost (JPY) | Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **食品衛生責任者 (Food Hygiene Manager)** | ~10,000 | 1-day lecture | Required for ALL food businesses. Book early -- lectures fill fast. |
| **飲食店営業許可 (Restaurant Business License)** | 8,900-18,300 | 2-3 weeks after application | Apply to local 保健所 (public health center); fee varies by ward. Apply ~2 weeks before construction completion. Facility inspection required. |
| **防火管理者 (Fire Prevention Manager)** | ~8,000 | 1-2 day course | Required if total capacity (staff + seats) >= 30 people. 乙種 (1 day) for spaces under 300 sqm. |
| **開業届 (Business Opening Notification)** | 0 (free) | Submit to tax office | Due within 1 month of opening. Enables 青色申告 (blue return) for tax benefits. |

### Conditionally Required

| Permit/License | Cost (JPY) | When Required |
|---|---|---|
| **菓子製造業許可 (Confectionery Manufacturing)** | 14,000-16,800 | If making and selling sweets/wagashi on-site |
| **深夜酒類提供飲食店届 (Late-Night Alcohol Service)** | 0 (free notification) | If serving alcohol past midnight |
| **酒類販売業免許 (Liquor Sales License)** | ~30,000 | If selling sealed bottles of alcohol to take away |

### Key Facility Requirements for 飲食店営業許可
- Separate kitchen area with swing door or partition
- 2-compartment sink (2槽シンク) with hot water
- Hand-washing sink separate from dish sink
- Adequate refrigeration with thermometer
- Proper ventilation
- Pest-proof construction (screens on windows, etc.)

## 8. Tokyo-Specific Considerations

### 商店街 (Shotengai / Shopping Streets) vs. 路面店 (Street-Facing)

| Factor | 商店街 | 路面大通り (Main Road) |
|---|---|---|
| **Rent** | 20-40% cheaper | Premium pricing |
| **Foot traffic** | Steady local traffic; loyal repeat customers | Higher volume but less targeted |
| **Vibe** | Local, community feel; good for specialty/niche | More commercial; brand visibility |
| **Regulations** | Some shotengai have tenant restrictions or aesthetic guidelines | Fewer restrictions |
| **Best for matcha cafe** | Strong fit for neighborhood specialty concept | Better for brand/chain expansion |

### Location Strategy Notes
- **Shimokitazawa, Koenji, Sangenjaya** shotengai areas offer the best rent-to-foot-traffic ratio for a niche specialty concept
- **Nakameguro, Daikanyama** suit premium positioning but require higher capital
- B1F/2F spaces work well for "destination" cafes with strong Instagram/social presence
- Proximity to a station exit matters enormously -- even 1 minute of walking distance significantly impacts rent and traffic
- Consider **Ekichika (駅チカ)** small spaces in renovated station buildings (e.g., Shimokitazawa's new station complex)

## 9. Real-World Examples & Benchmarks

### What Small Specialty Cafes Typically Spend to Open in Tokyo

| Cafe Type | Estimated Opening Cost (JPY) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Minimal indie cafe (居抜き, owner-operated, 8-10 tsubo)** | 4,000,000-7,000,000 | Used equipment; existing build-out; shotengai location |
| **Standard specialty cafe (15 tsubo, some renovation)** | 8,000,000-15,000,000 | Moderate build-out; new/mixed equipment; 1F location |
| **High-end specialty cafe (skeleton, 20 tsubo, custom design)** | 15,000,000-25,000,000+ | Full skeleton build; premium equipment; designer interior |

### Reference Points
- **Sarutahiko Coffee** started in Ebisu in 2011 as a small single-location specialty shop before expanding. Ebisu is a premium location (similar rent profile to Nakameguro/Daikanyama).
- **THE MATCHA TOKYO** operates in high-profile locations (Tokyo Solamachi, etc.) -- likely 20,000,000+ per location given premium positioning.
- **Atelier Matcha** (opened Aug 2021) is backed by a long-established tea wholesaler -- leverages existing supply chain.
- **Small independent matcha/tea shops** (like those in Yanaka, Kagurazaka, or Shimokitazawa) typically open in the 5,000,000-10,000,000 range using ikemuki properties.
- **Industry average** for cafe startups in metropolitan areas: 8,500,000-16,500,000 JPY.

### Key Success Patterns
1. **居抜き物件** is the most common cost-reduction strategy -- saves 50-70% on build-out
2. Many successful small cafes start with **total investment under 10,000,000 JPY** by using used equipment + ikemuki
3. Owner-operation for the first 1-2 years is standard to manage cash flow
4. Instagram/social media presence is essentially free marketing and critical for specialty cafes
5. Wholesale/retail matcha sales (online + in-store) provide higher-margin revenue alongside drink service

## 10. Business Registration Costs (開業届・法人設立)

### Option A: 個人事業主 (Sole Proprietor)

| Item | Cost (JPY) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **開業届 (business opening notification)** | 0 | Submit to local tax office within 1 month |
| **青色申告承認申請書** | 0 | Submit within 2 months of opening; enables up to 650,000 JPY tax deduction |
| **事業開始届 (prefectural tax)** | 0 | Submit to Tokyo metropolitan tax office |
| **Total** | **0** | All registrations are free |

### Option B: 合同会社 (LLC / Godo Kaisha)

| Item | Cost (JPY) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **定款作成 (articles of incorporation)** | 0 (self) or 50,000-100,000 (professional) | No notary authentication required for LLC |
| **収入印紙 (revenue stamp)** | 40,000 (paper) or 0 (electronic) | Electronic filing avoids stamp duty |
| **登録免許税 (registration tax)** | 60,000 | Minimum amount |
| **Total (self, electronic)** | **~60,000** | |
| **Total (with professional help)** | **100,000-200,000** | |

### Option C: 株式会社 (Corporation / Kabushiki Kaisha)

| Item | Cost (JPY) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **定款認証 (notarization)** | 30,000-50,000 | Varies by capital amount |
| **収入印紙 (revenue stamp)** | 40,000 (paper) or 0 (electronic) | |
| **登録免許税 (registration tax)** | 150,000 | Minimum amount |
| **謄本発行手数料** | ~2,000 | |
| **Total (self, electronic)** | **~200,000** | |
| **Total (with professional help)** | **250,000-400,000** | Including司法書士 (judicial scrivener) fees |

### Recommendation for a Small Matcha Cafe
- **Start as 個人事業主** -- zero cost, simpler tax filing, sufficient for a single location
- Consider 合同会社 if: taking on investors, need corporate contracts, or annual profit exceeds ~8,000,000 JPY (where corporate tax rate becomes advantageous)
- 株式会社 is overkill for initial opening; consider for Series A or multi-location expansion
