- Most quotes are **NNN (triple net)** or modified gross -- always clarify what's included
- **Realistic monthly range for a 400-600 sq ft cafe: $3,000-$6,000/month** in these neighborhoods

## 6. NYC-Specific Costs

### Commercial Rent Tax (CRT)
- Applies to tenants **south of 96th St in Manhattan** paying >$250k/year in rent
- Rate: 3.9% effective (6% on 65% of rent)
- **Exemption**: businesses with <$5M income and <$500k annual rent get full credit
- **Impact on a small cafe in Manhattan**: Likely exempt if rent is under ~$20k/month
- **Brooklyn locations**: NOT subject to CRT

### Private Waste Hauling
- NYC does NOT collect commercial garbage -- you must hire a private carter
- Max rate: $26.87/cubic yard (loose refuse)
- **Realistic monthly cost for small cafe: $300-$800/month**
- New Commercial Waste Zones program (rolling out 2025) may increase costs due to reduced competition

## 8. Blank Street Coffee: Low-Cost Model Analysis

### How They Kept Costs Low

| Strategy | Detail | Implication for You |
|---|---|---|
| **Micro-format stores** | 300-600 sq ft avg (started with 50 sq ft carts) | Lower rent; a 350 sq ft space vs 1,500 sq ft = ~75% rent savings |
| **Super-automatic machines** | Eversys Shotmaster Pro ($40-50k) | Higher upfront cost BUT reduces staff to 1-2 per shift (vs 5-6) |
| **Minimal staffing** | 1-2 baristas per shift | Dramatically lower labor costs (biggest ongoing expense) |
| **Started with carts** | $60-75k per cart to operate | Test market demand before committing to a lease |
| **Simple menu** | Limited offerings, no full kitchen | Smaller space needed, fewer permits, less equipment |
| **Scale purchasing** | Opened dozens of locations quickly | Bulk ingredient purchasing; not applicable to single-store |
| **Accessible pricing** | Drip coffee from $2.75 | Volume-driven model |

### Cart vs. Storefront Economics (Blank Street)

| | Cart | Small Storefront |
|---|---|---|
| **Startup cost** | $60,000-$75,000 | $150,000-$350,000 (est.) |
| **Monthly rent** | $0-$2,000 (permit-based) | $3,000-$6,000 |
| **Staff per shift** | 1 | 1-2 |
| **Revenue potential** | Lower (limited menu, weather-dependent) | Higher (year-round, more products) |

### Key Takeaway
Blank Street's model works because of **automation + tiny spaces + minimal labor**. The Eversys machine is expensive ($40-50k) but pays for itself by eliminating 2-3 staff positions per shift. For a single matcha-focused cafe, the lesson is: **invest in equipment that reduces labor dependency, and keep the space as small as operationally possible**.
