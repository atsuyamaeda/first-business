## 1. Commercial Lease Costs (300-800 sq ft)

### Monthly Rent by Neighborhood (per sq ft / year)

| Neighborhood | $/sq ft/year (estimate) | Monthly Rent (500 sq ft) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bushwick** | $35-55 | $1,460-$2,290 | Most affordable; raw/industrial spaces, growing demand |
| **Greenpoint** | $45-65 | $1,875-$2,710 | Gentrifying; popular with specialty food/bev |
| **Williamsburg** | $60-80 | $2,500-$3,330 | Avg $68/sq ft; one of priciest in Brooklyn |
| **Lower East Side** | $60-100 | $2,500-$4,170 | Manhattan premium; high foot traffic on key streets |
| **East Village** | $70-110 | $2,920-$4,580 | Premium location; strong cafe culture |

### Key Notes
- Small spaces (200-800 sq ft) tend to have **higher per-sq-ft rates** but lower total monthly rent
- Ground-floor retail with good frontage commands premium pricing
- Basement or second-floor spaces can be 30-50% cheaper
- Most quotes are **NNN (triple net)** or modified gross -- always clarify what's included
- **Realistic monthly range for a 400-600 sq ft cafe: $3,000-$6,000/month** in these neighborhoods

## 2. Key Money / Lease Deposits

| Item | Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Security deposit** | 2-6 months' rent (typical); up to 12 months for new businesses | Highly negotiable; depends on landlord risk assessment |
| **Key money** (lease assignment) | $0-$100,000+ | Legal in commercial leases; paid to outgoing tenant for below-market lease |
| **Personal guarantee** | Often required | Landlords may accept larger deposit in lieu of guarantor |
| **First + last month's rent** | Standard | Usually required at signing |
| **Broker fee** | 1 month's rent or % of lease value | If using a commercial broker |

### Realistic Deposit Budget
For a space at $4,500/month rent:
- Security deposit (3 months): **$13,500**
- First + last month: **$9,000**
- Broker fee: **$4,500-$9,000**
- **Total upfront lease costs: $27,000-$31,500** (without key money)

## 3. Build-Out / Renovation Costs

## 6. NYC-Specific Costs

### Commercial Rent Tax (CRT)
- Applies to tenants **south of 96th St in Manhattan** paying >$250k/year in rent
- Rate: 3.9% effective (6% on 65% of rent)
- **Exemption**: businesses with <$5M income and <$500k annual rent get full credit
- **Impact on a small cafe in Manhattan**: Likely exempt if rent is under ~$20k/month
- **Brooklyn locations**: NOT subject to CRT

### Private Waste Hauling
- NYC does NOT collect commercial garbage -- you must hire a private carter
- Max rate: $26.87/cubic yard (loose refuse)
- **Realistic monthly cost for small cafe: $300-$800/month**
- New Commercial Waste Zones program (rolling out 2025) may increase costs due to reduced competition

### Other NYC-Specific Costs
| Item | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Commercial insurance** | $3,000-$5,000/year | GL + workers' comp + property |
| **Workers' comp insurance** | ~$49/month per employee | Mandatory from first hire |

| **NY Secure Choice (retirement)** | Mandatory enrollment for 10+ employees (2026) | |

## 8. Blank Street Coffee: Low-Cost Model Analysis

### How They Kept Costs Low

| Strategy | Detail | Implication for You |
|---|---|---|
| **Micro-format stores** | 300-600 sq ft avg (started with 50 sq ft carts) | Lower rent; a 350 sq ft space vs 1,500 sq ft = ~75% rent savings |
| **Super-automatic machines** | Eversys Shotmaster Pro ($40-50k) | Higher upfront cost BUT reduces staff to 1-2 per shift (vs 5-6) |
| **Minimal staffing** | 1-2 baristas per shift | Dramatically lower labor costs (biggest ongoing expense) |
| **Started with carts** | $60-75k per cart to operate | Test market demand before committing to a lease |
| **Simple menu** | Limited offerings, no full kitchen | Smaller space needed, fewer permits, less equipment |
| **Scale purchasing** | Opened dozens of locations quickly | Bulk ingredient purchasing; not applicable to single-store |
| **Accessible pricing** | Drip coffee from $2.75 | Volume-driven model |

### Cart vs. Storefront Economics (Blank Street)

| | Cart | Small Storefront |
|---|---|---|
| **Startup cost** | $60,000-$75,000 | $150,000-$350,000 (est.) |
| **Monthly rent** | $0-$2,000 (permit-based) | $3,000-$6,000 |
| **Staff per shift** | 1 | 1-2 |
| **Revenue potential** | Lower (limited menu, weather-dependent) | Higher (year-round, more products) |

### Key Takeaway
Blank Street's model works because of **automation + tiny spaces + minimal labor**. The Eversys machine is expensive ($40-50k) but pays for itself by eliminating 2-3 staff positions per shift. For a single matcha-focused cafe, the lesson is: **invest in equipment that reduces labor dependency, and keep the space as small as operationally possible**.

## 9. Real-World Cafe Startup Examples

### Blank Street Coffee (NYC, est. 2020)
- **Started**: Mobile coffee cart at Wythe Diner, Williamsburg, Brooklyn (Aug 2020)
- **First cart cost**: ~$60,000-$75,000
- **Store format**: Micro-footprint 200-350 sq ft brick-and-mortar kiosks
- **Monthly rent per location**: ~$3,500 (vs ~$15,000 for traditional cafe)
- **Key investment**: Eversys Shotmaster Pro super-automatic espresso machine (~$50,000 per unit; makes 700 espressos/hour)
- **Staffing**: 1-2 baristas per shift (vs 5-6 for traditional cafe)
- **Total VC funding raised**: ~$100M (General Catalyst, Tiger Global, Warby Parker co-founder)
  - 2021 Seed: $7M
  - 2021 Series A: $25M (total $67M in 2021)
  - 2023 Series B: $20M
- **Lesson**: Automation + tiny spaces + minimal labor = dramatically lower overhead per location

### Junbi Matcha (franchise, California-based, expanding)
- **Franchise initial investment**: $268,750 - $576,250 (total range)
- **Franchise fee**: $30,000 (2024) / $35,000 (2025+)
- **Minimum liquid capital required**: $150,000
- **Ongoing fees**: 6% royalty on gross sales + 1% marketing fee
- **Lesson**: A matcha-focused cafe franchise considers $270K-$575K as the realistic range for a turnkey operation, which aligns with our Scenario B-C estimates above

### Cha Cha Matcha (NYC, est. 2016)
- No publicly disclosed investment figures
- Operates multiple locations in Manhattan (NoLiTa, Flatiron, etc.)
- Small-format, Instagram-forward design
- Estimated 400-600 sq ft per location
- Known for heavy branding investment and premium positioning

### MatchaBar (NYC, est. 2014, now Hustle brand)
- Originally Brooklyn-based matcha cafe
- Pivoted from cafe model to bottled RTD matcha beverages
- Illustrative of the challenge: cafe margins are thin, leading many to pivot to CPG/retail

## 12. Key Takeaways for Matcha Cafe Planning

1. **Most realistic budget for a first cafe**: $200,000-$350,000 (Brooklyn, 400-600 sq ft, moderate build-out)
2. **Monthly burn rate to plan for**: $25,000-$40,000/month
3. **Break-even timeline**: 18-27 months is realistic based on industry models
4. **Biggest cost levers**: Rent (location choice) and Labor (automation vs staff)
5. **Brooklyn advantage**: 30-50% lower rent than comparable Manhattan spaces
6. **Manhattan advantage**: Higher foot traffic and walk-in revenue potential
7. **Cart/kiosk as MVP**: $60,000-$100,000 to test market before committing to a lease (Blank Street playbook)
8. **Automation consideration**: A $40-50K super-automatic machine can eliminate 2-3 staff positions per shift, potentially saving $8,000-$15,000/month in labor
9. **Second-generation space**: Finding a previously-built-out cafe space can save $50,000-$100,000 on renovation
10. **Junbi benchmark**: A matcha-specific franchise considers $270K-$575K as the investment range — independent operation should target the lower half of this range
