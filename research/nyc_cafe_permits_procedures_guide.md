## 1. Business Entity Formation (LLC)

### Why LLC?
An LLC (Limited Liability Company) is the standard structure for NYC cafes. It separates personal assets from business liabilities and offers pass-through taxation.

### Formation Steps

| Step | Action | Cost | Timeline |
|------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | **Choose a business name** -- must be distinguishable from existing NY entities and include "LLC" | Free | 1 day |
| 2 | **File Articles of Organization** with NY Department of State (online or by mail) | $200 | 1-2 weeks (online is faster) |
| 3 | **Draft an Operating Agreement** -- required by NY law within 90 days of filing | $0-$1,500 (DIY or attorney) | 1-2 weeks |
| 4 | **Publication Requirement** -- publish notice of LLC formation in 2 newspapers (1 daily, 1 weekly) designated by the county clerk for 6 consecutive weeks | $800-$1,200 (NYC boroughs) | 6 weeks |
| 5 | **File Certificate of Publication** with NY Dept. of State after publication | $50 | 1 week |
| 6 | **Obtain EIN** (Employer Identification Number) from IRS -- required for tax filings, bank accounts, hiring | Free | Same day (online at irs.gov) |
| 7 | **Open a business bank account** using EIN + Articles of Organization | Free | 1-2 days |
| 8 | **Register for NY Sales Tax** -- get Certificate of Authority from NYS Dept. of Taxation & Finance | Free | 1-3 weeks |

### Critical Deadline
You must complete the **publication requirement within 120 days** of filing Articles of Organization, or the state will suspend your authority to conduct business.

### Total LLC Formation Cost: ~$1,050-$1,450+ (NYC)

## 2. Required Permits & Licenses

### A. Food Service Establishment Permit (DOHMH)

**Issuing Agency:** NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What it covers** | Required for ANY establishment serving food to the public |
| **Fee** | $280 (+ $25 if making frozen desserts) |
| **Application** | Online via NYC Business Express or in person at DCWP |
| **Key rule** | You may begin operating **22 days after submitting** your application, even before the permit is officially issued |
| **Inspection** | DOHMH will conduct an **unannounced pre-permit inspection** after you open |
| **Renewal** | Required periodically; all outstanding fines must be paid before renewal |

**Prerequisites before applying:**
- Proof of Certificate of Authority to Collect Sales Tax (address must match)
- Proof of workers' compensation and disability insurance (listing DOHMH as Certificate Holder)
- All outstanding health code violation fines paid

### B. Food Protection Certificate

**Issuing Agency:** NYC DOHMH (Health Academy)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Requirement** | At least ONE certified food protection supervisor must be on-site at ALL times during operation |
| **Online course** | Free, self-paced, 15 lessons (English, Spanish, Chinese). Final exam is in-person at Health Academy (160 W 100th St, Manhattan) |
| **In-person course** | $114, 15 hours over 5 days (Mon-Fri, 3 hrs/day) |
| **Exam fee** | $24.60 (for online course path) |
| **Expiration** | Certificate does NOT expire -- lifetime validity |
| **Languages** | Study guide and exam available in English + 36 other languages |

**Recommendation:** Complete this early -- it's free online and never expires. Get it during the pre-lease planning phase.

### C. Business Certificate (DBA / "Doing Business As")

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **When needed** | If operating under a name different from your LLC's legal name |
| **Filed with** | County Clerk's office in the county where the business operates |
| **Cost** | ~$100-$120 |
| **Timeline** | Same day to 1 week |

**Note:** If your LLC name IS your trade name (e.g., "Duality Matcha LLC" operating as "Duality Matcha"), you may not need a separate DBA.

### D. Sales Tax Certificate of Authority

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Issuing agency** | NYS Department of Taxation and Finance |
| **What it does** | Authorizes you to collect sales tax on taxable goods/services |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **Application** | Online via NY Business Express or Form DTF-17 |
| **Timeline** | 1-3 weeks to receive |
| **Required before** | Opening for business AND applying for DOHMH food permit |

**NYC sales tax rate (2025):** 8.875% (4% state + 4.5% city + 0.375% MCTD surcharge)

### E. Employer Identification Number (EIN)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Issuing agency** | IRS (federal) |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **Application** | Online at irs.gov (instant) or via Form SS-4 (mail/fax: 4-5 weeks) |
| **Required for** | Opening bank accounts, filing taxes, hiring employees, applying for permits |

### F. Certificate of Occupancy (CO) / Letter of No Objection (LNO)

**Issuing Agency:** NYC Department of Buildings (DOB)

This is one of the most complex and time-consuming steps.

| Scenario | Required Document | Timeline |
|----------|-------------------|----------|
| Space already has restaurant/cafe CO | May not need new CO -- verify with DOB | 1-2 weeks to verify |
| Converting retail to cafe (pre-1938 building, <75 occupancy) | **Letter of No Objection (LNO)** -- simpler process | 4-8 weeks |
| Converting retail to cafe (post-1938 building or >75 occupancy) | **New Certificate of Occupancy (Alt-1 application)** | 6-10 weeks for review + construction time |
| Major renovation / change in egress | **New CO required** via Alt-1 filing | 3-6 months |

**Key considerations:**
- Check the **existing CO** before signing any lease -- if the space is already permitted for food service, this saves months
- Converting retail to restaurant requires DOB review of zoning, egress, and ADA accessibility
- DOB inspection is required after construction to issue or amend the CO
- Must also pass FDNY fire safety inspection

### G. Additional Permits & Registrations

| Permit/License | Agency | Cost | Notes |
|----------------|--------|------|-------|
| **Workers' Compensation Insurance** | Private carrier (required by NYS) | Varies ($2,000-$5,000+/yr) | Must have BEFORE applying for food permit |
| **Disability Insurance** | Private carrier or NYS Insurance Fund | ~$500-$1,500/yr | Required for all employers |
| **Signage Permit** | DOB | $100-$300 | Required for exterior signs; must comply with zoning |
| **Sidewalk Cafe / Outdoor Dining Permit** | DOT / DCWP (Open Restaurants program) | Varies | If you want outdoor seating |
| **Commercial Waste Removal** | Private hauler (NYC Commercial Waste Zones) | $200-$500/mo | Must use designated zone hauler as of 2025 |
| **Music License (ASCAP/BMI/SESAC)** | Private licensing orgs | $300-$800/yr | If playing background music |
| **Grease Trap Compliance** | DEP | Part of build-out | Required for food establishments with cooking |
| **Fire Suppression System** | FDNY | Part of build-out | Required for commercial cooking (hood + Ansul system) |
| **Tobacco Retail Dealer License** | DCWP | $200 biennial | Only if selling tobacco products |
| **Liquor License (SLA)** | NYS Liquor Authority | $4,352+ | Only if serving alcohol; 4-6 month process with Community Board review |

## 3. Health Department Inspections & Grading

### Inspection Cycle

1. **Pre-Permit Inspection** -- Unannounced, occurs after you open (within 22 days of permit application). Not graded. Verifies basic compliance.

2. **Annual Cycle Inspections** -- Every permitted establishment receives at least one unscheduled inspection per year.

3. **Re-Inspections** -- If initial cycle inspection scores in B or C range, an unannounced re-inspection follows within approximately one month.

### Letter Grading System

| Grade | Score (Points) | Meaning |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **A** | 0-13 | Excellent -- minimal violations |
| **B** | 14-27 | Good -- some violations noted |
| **C** | 28+ | Needs improvement -- significant violations |

**How scoring works:**
- Violations carry point values (lower = better)
- Critical violations (e.g., evidence of mice/roaches, improper food temperature) carry higher points
- General violations (e.g., improper signage, minor facility issues) carry lower points

### Two-Inspection Process

| Phase | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **Initial Inspection** | Inspector scores the establishment but does NOT assign a public grade |
| If score is 0-13 (A range) | Grade A posted immediately; no re-inspection needed |
| If score is 14+ (B or C range) | Re-inspection scheduled within ~1 month |
| **Re-Inspection** | Restaurant receives its official letter grade based on this score |

### Grade Posting Requirements
- Grade card **must be posted** in a visible location to passersby (typically front window)
- Failure to post is itself a violation
- Grades are also published on the [NYC Restaurant Inspection Results](https://a816-health.nyc.gov/ABCEatsRestaurants/) public database

### Common Violations to Prepare For
- Food held at improper temperature (above 41F or below 140F)
- Evidence of mice, roaches, or flies
- Inadequate handwashing facilities
- Improper food storage (cross-contamination risk)
- Lack of Food Protection Certificate holder on premises
- Improper sanitization of food-contact surfaces

### Inspector Qualifications
Each inspector holds a bachelor's degree + 30 credit hours in natural sciences, and completes 4-6 months of field training before conducting solo inspections.

## 4. Lease Negotiation (NYC-Specific)

### Critical Lease Terms for a Cafe

#### a) Lease Term & Renewal Options
- **Target:** 10-15 year primary term (minimum 10 for a cafe with significant build-out)
- **Renewal options:** Negotiate at least 1-2 five-year renewal options at predetermined rent increases
- **Why:** Build-out costs for a cafe ($100K-$300K+) require years to recoup; a short lease is a business killer

#### b) Personal Guarantee vs. Good Guy Guarantee

| Type | What It Means | Risk to You |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| **Full Personal Guarantee** | You are personally liable for ALL rent through the entire lease term, even if the business fails | Very high -- can mean $500K+ personal liability |
| **Good Guy Guarantee** (NYC-specific) | You are personally liable ONLY until you vacate and return keys, provided you are current on rent at time of surrender | Much lower -- standard in NYC; negotiate for this |
| **Burn-Off Clause** | Personal guarantee expires after X months/years of on-time rent payments | Best outcome -- try to negotiate 12-24 month burn-off |

**Negotiation priority:** Always push for a Good Guy Guarantee with a burn-off clause. Full personal guarantees should be a dealbreaker for first-time operators.

#### c) Key Money

- Key money (a one-time payment to the landlord or previous tenant for the right to occupy the space) is **less common in NYC than in Tokyo** but still exists in high-demand locations
- More common when taking over an existing restaurant space with existing build-out (paying for the "keys" to an operating kitchen)
- Typical range: $10,000-$100,000+ for prime locations
- **Negotiate:** Key money is entirely negotiable and should be weighed against build-out savings

#### d) Lease Assignment & Sublease

- **Critical clause:** Ensure the lease allows assignment with landlord consent "not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed"
- Without this, you **cannot sell your business** (the lease is the business's most valuable asset)
- Negotiate that upon assignment to a "qualified assignee" (equally capitalized operator), the original tenant is released from future liability
- Some landlords demand a right of first refusal or "recapture" clause -- resist these as they kill your resale value

#### e) Use Clause
- Negotiate a **broadly worded use clause**: "cafe, restaurant, retail sale of food and beverages, and related ancillary uses"
- Avoid narrow clauses like "matcha cafe only" -- you may want to add retail merchandise, events, or other items later
- Ensure the use clause is compatible with the building's Certificate of Occupancy

#### f) Rent Escalation
- Fixed annual increases of 2-3% are standard
- Avoid CPI-linked escalations (can spike unpredictably)
- Some leases include "step-ups" (e.g., rent is lower in years 1-2 during build-out, then increases)

#### g) Build-Out & Tenant Improvement (TI) Allowance
- Negotiate for a **TI allowance** ($20-$80/sq ft) or free rent period (2-4 months) to offset build-out costs
- Ensure the lease specifies who owns the improvements (tenant or landlord) at lease end
- Get landlord's written consent for all planned renovations before signing

#### h) Exclusive Use Clause
- If in a multi-tenant building, negotiate an **exclusive use clause** preventing the landlord from leasing to a competing cafe/tea shop in the same building or adjacent spaces

## 5. Zoning: Brooklyn vs Manhattan

### NYC Zoning Basics

NYC is divided into three zoning district types:
- **R** (Residential)
- **C** (Commercial)
- **M** (Manufacturing)

A cafe falls under **Use Group 6** (restaurants, retail, personal services), which is permitted in:
- Most **C** (Commercial) districts: C1, C2, C3, C4, C5, C6, C7, C8
- All **M** (Manufacturing) districts
- **C1/C2 overlay** districts within residential zones (common in Brooklyn)

### Brooklyn Zoning Considerations

| Neighborhood | Typical Zoning | Notes |
|-------------|---------------|-------|
| **Williamsburg** | Mixed C4/M1/R6 with commercial overlays | M1 zones allow cafes; former industrial spaces popular but may need CO change |
| **Bushwick** | M1 (manufacturing) transitioning | Cafes allowed in M1; watch for rezoning proposals that could affect rent |
| **Greenpoint** | C4/M1 mix | Similar to Williamsburg; waterfront areas may have special restrictions |
| **Park Slope** | C1/C2 overlays on residential | Small-scale commercial allowed along main avenues (5th Ave, 7th Ave) |
| **DUMBO** | Mixed use / special district | Check specific lot zoning; some areas have height/use restrictions |

**Brooklyn advantages:**
- Lower rents per square foot
- More available M1/manufacturing spaces that allow food service
- Growing foot traffic in gentrifying neighborhoods
- More lenient landlords (flexible on lease terms for new businesses)

### Manhattan Zoning Considerations

| Neighborhood | Typical Zoning | Notes |
|-------------|---------------|-------|
| **Lower East Side** | C6/C1-C2 overlays | Densely commercial; most spaces already zoned for food service |
| **East Village** | C1/C2 overlays, some R zones | Verify zoning lot by lot; some residential blocks restrict commercial use |
| **West Village** | Historic district overlays | Landmarks Preservation Commission (LPC) review may be required for exterior changes |
| **SoHo/NoHo** | Recent rezoning (2024) | New mixed-use rules; check current status carefully |
| **Midtown** | C5/C6 (high-density commercial) | Very expensive but high foot traffic; signage regulations may be strict |

**Manhattan advantages:**
- Higher foot traffic density
- Established cafe culture and customer base
- Better transit access
- Higher average ticket price tolerance

### How to Check Zoning for a Specific Address

1. Go to **[NYC ZoLa (Zoning & Land Use Map)](https://zola.planning.nyc.gov/)**
2. Enter the address
3. Verify the zoning district and allowed use groups
4. Check for any special district overlays or landmarks restrictions

## 6. Master Timeline: Lease Signing to Opening

### Realistic Timeline: 6-12 Months Total

| Phase | Timeframe | Duration | Key Activities |
|-------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| **Pre-Lease** | Months -3 to 0 | 3 months | Business plan, LLC formation, EIN, Food Protection Certificate, location scouting, zoning verification |
| **Lease Negotiation** | Month 0-1 | 2-6 weeks | Negotiate terms, attorney review, sign lease |
| **Design & Architecture** | Months 1-3 | 6-10 weeks | Hire architect, design space, prepare DOB filing drawings |
| **DOB Permits** | Months 2-4 | 4-10 weeks | File for construction permits (Alt-1 or Alt-2), CO/LNO review, expediter recommended |
| **Construction / Build-Out** | Months 4-6 | 6-12 weeks | Demo, plumbing, electrical, HVAC, finishes. Inspections throughout |
| **Pre-Opening Permits** | Month 5-6 | 2-4 weeks | Apply for Food Service Establishment Permit, signage, insurance, waste hauler |
| **DOB Final Inspection** | Month 6 | 1-2 weeks | DOB sign-off, FDNY inspection, CO issued or amended |
| **Soft Opening** | Month 6-7 | 1-2 weeks | Open 22 days after food permit application; DOHMH pre-permit inspection (unannounced) |
| **Grand Opening** | Month 7+ | -- | Full operations, marketing launch |

### Parallel Tracks (Do These Simultaneously)

```
TRACK A: Legal & Business Setup     TRACK B: Space & Construction      TRACK C: Operations Prep
------------------------------      -------------------------           ----------------------
LLC formation (Month -3)            Location scouting (Month -2)        Food Protection Cert (Month -3)
EIN (Month -3)                      Zoning verification (Month -1)      Menu development (Month -2)
Sales Tax Certificate (Month -2)    Lease signing (Month 0)             Equipment sourcing (Month 1)
Workers' Comp Insurance (Month 0)   Architect + design (Month 1-3)      Supplier contracts (Month 2)
DBA if needed (Month 0)             DOB permits (Month 2-4)             Staff hiring (Month 4)
Food Permit Application (Month 5)   Construction (Month 4-6)            Staff training (Month 5)
                                    DOB final inspection (Month 6)      POS / systems setup (Month 5)
```

### Common Delays & How to Avoid Them

| Delay | Typical Impact | Mitigation |
|-------|---------------|------------|
| DOB permit review backlog | +2-6 weeks | Hire a professional expediter ($3,000-$8,000) |
| CO change of use required | +2-4 months | Choose a space that already has food-service CO |
| Construction surprises (old building) | +2-8 weeks | Budget 20% contingency; do thorough inspection before signing lease |
| Plumbing/grease trap issues | +2-4 weeks | Verify existing plumbing capacity before lease |
| FDNY inspection failure | +1-3 weeks | Hire an FPE (Fire Protection Engineer) during design phase |

## 7. Step-by-Step Checklist

### Phase 0: Pre-Lease (3 months before lease signing)

- [ ] Write business plan and financial projections
- [ ] Form LLC (file Articles of Organization with NY Dept. of State)
- [ ] Complete LLC publication requirement (6 weeks in 2 newspapers)
- [ ] Obtain EIN from IRS
- [ ] Register for NYS Sales Tax Certificate of Authority
- [ ] Complete Food Protection Certificate (free online course + in-person exam)
- [ ] Open business bank account
- [ ] Begin location scouting; verify zoning for each candidate space
- [ ] Assemble advisory team: attorney (commercial lease), architect, accountant

### Phase 1: Lease & Design (Months 0-3)

- [ ] Verify zoning and existing Certificate of Occupancy for target space
- [ ] Negotiate lease (prioritize: Good Guy Guarantee, assignment rights, broad use clause, TI allowance)
- [ ] Have attorney review lease before signing
- [ ] Sign lease
- [ ] Obtain workers' compensation and disability insurance
- [ ] Hire architect experienced with NYC restaurant build-outs
- [ ] Complete space design and DOB filing drawings
- [ ] File for DOB construction permits (Alt-1 or Alt-2)

### Phase 2: Construction & Permits (Months 3-6)

- [ ] Obtain DOB permit approval
- [ ] Begin construction / build-out
- [ ] Schedule required DOB inspections (building, plumbing, electrical)
- [ ] Install grease trap, fire suppression system (hood + Ansul), HVAC
- [ ] Pass FDNY fire safety inspection
- [ ] Obtain Certificate of Occupancy or Letter of No Objection
- [ ] Apply for Food Service Establishment Permit ($280)
- [ ] Apply for signage permit (DOB)
- [ ] Set up commercial waste hauler (designated zone)
- [ ] Obtain music license (ASCAP/BMI) if applicable

### Phase 3: Pre-Opening (Month 6)

- [ ] Hire and train staff
- [ ] Set up POS system, accounting, payroll
- [ ] Finalize supplier contracts and first inventory order
- [ ] Post required signage: occupancy limit, choking poster, hand-washing signs, no-smoking signs
- [ ] Ensure Food Protection Certificate holder scheduled for all operating hours
- [ ] Register for NYC commercial rent tax (if applicable -- Manhattan south of 96th St, annual rent > $250K)

### Phase 4: Opening (Month 6-7)

- [ ] Begin operations 22 days after food permit application submission
- [ ] Prepare for unannounced DOHMH pre-permit inspection
- [ ] Post letter grade when received
- [ ] Monitor and comply with all ongoing regulatory requirements
- [ ] File for sidewalk cafe permit if desired (separate process, 5-6 months)
